Northwest Registered Agent Review (2022)

We might receive compensation from the companies whose products we review. The opinions here are our own.

Northwest Registered Agent, also known as just “Northwest”, is an online provider of business services.

As the name suggests, Northwest is primarily a registered agent service. Entrepreneurs can select Northwest as their company's registered agent, which means that they will handle legal correspondence and other important documents.

In many states, having a registered agent is a requirement for forming certain business structures. Some states allow you to be your own registered agent, but many entrepreneurs choose to hire a company in order to simplify things.

Ongoing costs

Many business owners will only pay for the upfront costs of using Northwest to form a new business. However, it's worth noting that some of Northwest's services are ongoing, and may incur ongoing costs.

For instance, the Registered Agent service $125 per year, while the Annual Report filing service costs $100 per year.

Completely optional add-ons like a virtual office, business VoIP phone number, or mail forwarding also have ongoing costs.

For basic business formation, you won't necessarily need any of these ongoing services.

What about state filing fees?

Northwest charges state filing fees separately. This is standard practice in the industry.

Fees vary by state, as well as by the business type (LLC, corporation, or nonprofit). Some states are as low as $50, while others are several hundred dollars.

During the signup process, Northwest will show you the total cost, including state fees. Keep in mind that 100% of these state filing fees go directly to your state, although Northwest facilitates the transaction.

Does Northwest Registered Agent have good customer service?

Northwest Registered Agent has excellent customer support. In fact, it's a major selling point of their business model.

When a customer signs up for Northwest, they will be paired with their own Corporate Guide. This guide helps facilitate the setup process and is there to answer any questions. Even long after the initial sign up, the corporate guide will be available to answer questions and resolve issues.

This differs from the traditional customer service model, in which customers call in and are paired with whatever agent is available.
